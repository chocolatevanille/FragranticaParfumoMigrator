Prodigy — Mind Games

Prodigy — Mind Games

I tend to find Mind Games as a house is a little overrated. All of their fragrances are above average to be sure but $400 a pop is a *lot* of money and I wasn't feeling the hype that others were about most of them. Needless to say I wasn't expecting much when I heard they have a new pair coming in at half a grand each.

Now that I have not just smelled Prodigy on paper but also worn it on skin for a couple days, I can say without a doubt that 100% I was wrong, this fragrance actually is that good and if you haven't experienced it you need to go do so right now. It's not even fair to call it good; it's great, or probably whatever comes after great. Brilliant? That sounds right.

The rose mixed with the blonde woods and sandalwood give it a strong but warm earthy base, and the sweetness of the vanilla and cardamom pull those aromas together into something of an earthy gourmand. Scrolling through these reviews, many have likened it to some variety of latte. I want to emphasize that this is not a coffee fragrance, but the woods and patchouli give it the rich earthiness you might identify with coffee and the vanilla and cardamom the warmth and sweetness of the creamer.

The drydown is an elegant deconstruction of the blend: the vanilla bounds forward and takes most of the attention, appropriately as after a long day the soft vanilla is something of a comfort. Patchouli and sandalwood work together to maintain an earthy feel. Most importantly for me, the drydown is exquisitely smooth. There's no roughness whatsoever.

With the disclosure that rose, vanilla, and sandalwood are three of my favourite notes ever, I'd go as far as to say this is one of the best fragrances I have ever experienced.

Tyrannosaurus Rex — Zoologist Perfumes

Tyrannosaurus Rex — Zoologist Perfumes

First impression: Wow, this is dirty. Almost has a body odor flavor to it, but it's much spicier and can almost sting my nose if I smell it directly (going to attribute this to the strong incense). My initial reaction was repulsion, but after giving it maybe 20 minutes to settle, I have to say it is growing on me. The sillage is more pleasant than smelling it directly (although it is still very bold and no doubt polarizing).

End of day thoughts: T-Rex evolves nicely over the day. The drydown is calmer, less dirty and more sweet. The incense is still in full effect though, and if smelled up close it still packs a punch. The projection was also very strong for me, stronger even than Tobacco Vanille or Tuscan Leather. Projection lasted around 4 hours before moving into arm's length. The scent is still with me 11 hours after application.

After some reflection, I believe the initial spiciness of it is due to a strong burning smell. I had the thought that this was very close to John Varvatos Dark Rebel Rider, a fragrance I've had in my wardrobe for about half a year. Lo and behold, DRR has resins and incense as its number 2 and 3 most strong notes here on Fragrantica, respectively. T-Rex has them at 1 and 2. They're also both heavy on leather. I'd wager you can get a pretty good idea of whether you would enjoy this scent from DRR which almost takes away from T-Rex given its price comparatively.

Overall, a job well done by Zoologist I would say. Bold, dirty, animalic, but still somewhat wearable. Perhaps most importantly, I could easily be convinced that this is what a T-Rex smells like!
